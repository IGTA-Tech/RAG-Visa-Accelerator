# O-1 Visa Eligibility Structure: Understanding the Two Talent Pools

## Executive Summary

We work with two distinct groups of international tech talent. Understanding the difference is crucial for structuring our partnership effectively. This document outlines who qualifies for immediate O-1 filing versus who needs development, and how each group integrates into your operations.

---

## The O-1 Visa Reality

The O-1 visa requires demonstrating "extraordinary ability" through meeting at least 3 of 8 specific criteria. Not everyone qualifies immediately - there's a clear line between those ready NOW and those who need time to build their profile.

---

## GROUP A: O-1 READY (Immediate Filing)

### Who They Are
- **Current Status:** Senior engineers at FAANG, unicorns, or established startups
- **Visa Situation:** Usually on H-1B facing renewal issues, layoff risk, or wanting flexibility
- **Experience Level:** 5-10+ years with demonstrable impact
- **AI Score:** 85%+ on our evaluation (www.aivisaevaluation.com)

### What Makes Them O-1 Ready
They already have evidence for 3+ criteria:
- **Published work:** GitHub contributions, technical blogs, papers
- **Critical role:** Lead engineer titles, architecture ownership
- **High salary:** Previous W-2s showing top-tier compensation
- **Industry recognition:** Conference talks, open source maintainer status
- **Press coverage:** Featured in TechCrunch, company engineering blogs
- **Peer review:** Code review leadership, technical interviewer roles

### What They Need From Partners
- **Deal Memos:** Letters expressing interest in potential engagement
- **No actual work required:** Just acknowledgment of their expertise
- **Timeline:** Can file O-1 within 30-60 days
- **Risk Level:** ZERO - these are proven professionals

## GROUP B: NOT YET ELIGIBLE (Development Needed)

### Who They Are
- **Current Status:** Mid-level engineers with solid skills but limited visibility
- **Visa Situation:** F-1 OPT, early-stage H-1B, or abroad
- **Experience Level:** 2-5 years, good technical skills
- **AI Score:** 60-84% on our evaluation

### What They're Missing
Need to build evidence for O-1 criteria:
- **No publications:** Haven't written technical content
- **Limited recognition:** Work within companies, not publicly visible  
- **Standard roles:** "Software Engineer II" not "Lead" or "Principal"
- **No speaking:** Haven't presented at conferences
- **Internal impact:** Great work but not externally validated

### What They Need From Partners
- **Real projects:** Actual work to showcase skills
- **Public credit:** Named contributions, case studies
- **Timeline:** 6-8 months of profile building
- **Parallel work:** Shadow your team while building portfolio

## The Qualification System

### How We Determine Eligibility

**Our AI Evaluation (www.aivisaevaluation.com):**
1. **Analyzes actual work:** GitHub commits, code quality, project complexity
2. **Scores 8 O-1 criteria:** Automatically identifies qualifying evidence
3. **Provides percentage score:** 85%+ = Ready, 60-84% = Accelerator, <60% = Not viable
4. **Processes at scale:** 100+ evaluations daily with 85-90% accuracy

### Why This Matters
- **Saves your time:** Only see pre-qualified candidates
- **Reduces risk:** Know exactly who's eligible before engagement
- **Clear expectations:** No surprises about visa readiness
- **Efficient pipeline:** Focus on talent that can actually work

## Operational Structure

### For O-1 Ready Candidates

**Week 1: Initial Review**
- We send 5-10 qualified profiles
- Each includes AI score and evidence summary
- You select which to support (or support all)

**Week 2: Documentation**
- We provide interest letter template
- You customize with your details
- Letters issued in batch

**Week 3-8: Visa Processing**
- We file O-1 petitions
- Handle all USCIS requirements
- Keep you updated on progress

**Post-Approval:**
- Candidates available for engagement
- You decide if/when to use them
- Multiple employer model gives flexibility

### For Development Candidates

**Month 1: Matching**
- We identify candidates needing specific experience
- Match with your technology stack
- Establish shadow/parallel work structure

**Months 2-6: Building**
- Candidates work on non-critical projects
- Generate technical content about work
- Build public portfolio with your permission

**Month 7: Transition**
- Re-evaluate with AI scoring
- Graduate to O-1 Ready status
- Begin visa filing process

## Why Two Groups Matter

### The Reality Check
- **Only 15-20% of applicants are O-1 ready** immediately

- Mixing the groups creates confusion and risk

### Our Recommendation

2. **Test 1-2 development candidates** - See if parallel work model fits
3. **Scale based on results** - Expand what works for you

## The Bottom Line

**O-1 Ready candidates** need recognition, not training. They're proven professionals who just need documentation of opportunities.

**Development candidates** need experience and visibility. They're future talent who can contribute while building their profile.

Understanding this distinction ensures we send you the right people for the right purposes, maximizing value while minimizing complexity.
