# VISA PROCESS GUIDE

## OVERVIEW: THE SSV CLIENT JOURNEY

### Stage 1: Initial Contact
1. Lead contacts SSV (website, referral, social media)
2. BD team sends intake form request
3. Lead completes self-evaluation at sherrodsportsvisas.report

### Stage 2: Evaluation
1. Attorney reviews intake form and documents
2. Assessment of visa eligibility
3. Visa type recommendation (P-1, O-1, or EB-1A)
4. Qualification determination

### Stage 3: Pricing & Engagement
1. Send pricing based on visa type
2. Client reviews terms
3. Client signs engagement letter
4. Client submits payment

### Stage 4: Document Collection
1. Client receives document checklist
2. Client gathers evidence
3. SSV reviews and requests additional docs
4. Case preparation begins

### Stage 5: Petition Filing
1. SSV prepares petition package
2. Final review with client
3. File with USCIS
4. Receive receipt notice

### Stage 6: Adjudication
1. Wait for USCIS decision
2. Respond to any RFEs (Requests for Evidence)
3. Receive approval/denial
4. If approved: Proceed to consular processing (if abroad)

### Stage 7: Visa Issuance
1. Schedule consular interview (if applicable)
2. Attend interview
3. Receive visa stamp
4. Enter United States

## P-1 VISA PROCESS SPECIFICS

### Timeline
- Standard Processing: 3-6 months
- Premium Processing: 15 business days (add $2,805)

### Required Documents
1. Passport (valid 6+ months)
2. Competition/event itinerary
3. Contracts or offer letters
4. Evidence of international recognition:
   - Rankings
   - Media coverage
   - Awards
   - National team letters
5. Employer/agent petitioner documents

### Key Requirements
- Must have specific US event/competition
- Petitioner (employer or agent) required
- International recognition in sport

### P-1 vs P-1S
- **P-1A**: Primary athlete
- **P-1S**: Essential support personnel (coaches, trainers accompanying P-1A)

---

## O-1 VISA PROCESS SPECIFICS

### Timeline
- Standard Processing: 3-6 months
- Premium Processing: 15 business days (add $2,805)

### Required Documents
1. Passport (valid 6+ months)
2. Evidence meeting 3+ criteria:
   - Awards/prizes
   - Memberships in associations
   - Published material about you
   - Judging experience
   - Original contributions
   - Scholarly articles
   - High salary evidence
   - Critical role documentation
3. Petitioner documents
4. Advisory opinion (sometimes required)

### O-1A vs O-1B
- **O-1A**: Athletes, scientists, educators, business
- **O-1B**: Artists, entertainers (different criteria, often easier for influencers)

### Advantages Over P-1
- More flexibility (not tied to specific event)
- Can work for multiple employers
- 3-year initial validity
- Better for coaching, training, influencing

## EB-1A PROCESS SPECIFICS

### Timeline
- Standard Processing: 6-12 months
- Premium Processing: Available (15 business days initial response)
- Total to Green Card: 12-18 months typical

### Required Documents
1. All standard immigration documents
2. Evidence meeting 3+ of 10 criteria
3. Detailed personal statement
4. Recommendation letters
5. Extensive documentation of achievements

### Key Differences from O-1
- **Result**: Permanent residence (green card)
- **Standard**: Higher (top of field, not just extraordinary)
- **Benefit**: Path to citizenship, spouse can work

## PETITIONER SERVICES EXPLAINED

### What is a Petitioner?
The US entity (employer, agent, or organization) that sponsors the visa application on behalf of the foreign national.

### When is Petitioner Service Needed?
- Client has no US employer
- Client wants to work independently
- Client's employer won't file
- Agent representation preferred

### How SSV Provides Petitioner Services
Through two affiliated entities:
1. **Aventus Visa Agents (AVA)**: Standard sports cases
2. **Innovative Global Talent Agency (IGTA)**: Complex/entertainment cases

### Cost
- $2,000 flat fee
- Added to base visa service fee
- Example: O-1 ($8,000) + Petitioner ($2,000) = $10,000

### FRM Exception
First Round Management clients self-petition, so they don't need the $2,000 petitioner service.

## PREMIUM PROCESSING

### What It Is
Expedited USCIS review with guaranteed response in 15 business days.

### Cost
$2,805 (paid directly to USCIS)

### When to Recommend
- Urgent competition/event dates
- Expiring current status
- Time-sensitive job offers
- Client preference for speed

### What It Guarantees
- Initial response within 15 business days
- Response can be: Approval, Denial, or RFE
- If RFE issued, clock resets after response

## CONSULAR PROCESSING

### What It Is
The process of obtaining the actual visa stamp at a US Embassy/Consulate abroad after petition approval.

### When Required
- Client is outside the US
- Client needs to travel and re-enter

### Process Steps
1. Petition approved by USCIS
2. Case sent to National Visa Center (NVC)
3. Schedule embassy interview
4. Attend interview with documents
5. Receive visa stamp in passport
6. Enter US within visa validity

### Key Documents for Interview
- Passport
- Approval notice (I-797)
- DS-160 confirmation
- Financial documents
- Supporting evidence

## COMMON QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### Q: How long does the process take?
**A:** Standard processing is 3-6 months. Premium processing guarantees a response in 15 business days for an additional $2,805.

### Q: Can I work while my visa is processing?
**A:** Not on the new visa until approved. If you have current valid status, you may continue working under that.

### Q: What if I get denied?
**A:** We can review the denial reasons and determine if an appeal, motion to reopen, or new filing is appropriate.

### Q: Can my family come with me?
**A:** Yes. O-1 holders can bring spouse/children on O-3 visas ($1,000 additional per dependent).

### Q: Do I need a job offer?
**A:** For P-1, yes - you need a specific event/competition. For O-1, you need a petitioner but can have more flexibility in the work scope.

### Q: What's the difference between P-1 and O-1?
**A:** 
- P-1: For specific competitions/events, tied to that petitioner
- O-1: More flexible, can work for multiple employers, better for long-term presence

### Q: Can I change employers on O-1?
**A:** Yes, but a new petition must be filed by the new employer.

### Q: How long can I stay?
**A:** 
- P-1: Duration of event plus 30 days
- O-1: 3 years initially, can extend
- EB-1A: Permanent (green card)

## RFE (REQUEST FOR EVIDENCE) HANDLING

### What It Is
USCIS request for additional documentation before making a decision.

### Common RFE Topics
- Insufficient evidence of achievements
- Need for advisory opinion
- Clarification of job duties
- Additional proof of recognition

### SSV's Approach
1. Analyze RFE carefully
2. Gather additional evidence
3. Prepare comprehensive response
4. File within deadline (typically 84 days)

### Impact on Timeline
- Standard: Adds 2-4 months
- Premium: Clock resets; 15 days after RFE response
